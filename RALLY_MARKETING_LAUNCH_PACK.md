## 1. Brand voice guide (the one page)

**Who's talking:** a female fan who is *actually* a fan — not here with her partner, not asking permission, funnier than you. That's the whole brand.

**Register:** deadpan verdicts. Short declaratives. One period per sentence — the period is the punchline landing. She never explains the joke.

**The viral test — every piece of copy, every post, must pass at least 3 of 4:**
1. Screenshot-able (works as a still image with zero context)
2. Second layer (fans get something casuals don't)
3. Sounds like speech (you can hear a real woman saying it)
4. Specific (a detail only someone who watches would include)

**Do:** talk directly to him ("I DON'T NEED YOU TO MANSPLAIN."), bet on her competence, let the garment be the straight man, use sports slang correctly and casually (knowing ball is the brand).
**Don't:** wine-mom energy, "sorry not sorry," explaining jokes in captions, team names/logos/athlete names, "Official," MARCH MADNESS / FINAL FOUR / SUPER BOWL / SATURDAYS ARE FOR THE… / WORLD SERIES, sportsbook brand names, punctuation drift (no exclamation points — excitement is for amateurs; she's been right before).

**Caption formula:** the shirt is the headline; the caption is the second beat, not a repeat. Shirt: HOT GIRLS KNOW BALL. → Caption: "it's on a shirt now so it's official."

## 3. Email flows

**Launch announcement (list + friends):**
- Subject: `You know ball. Now there's proof.`
- Preheader: `Drop 1 is live — 30+ designs for women who actually watch.`
- Body: For every woman who's been asked "do you even watch?" — Drop 1 is our answer. Shirts that know ball, panties that are lucky, and nothing — nothing — with a cutesy font. Made for the fan, not the fan's girlfriend. → SHOP DROP 1
- P.S.: `The parlay shirts are selling like the parlay hit. Because it did.`

**Welcome flow (3 emails, new subscribers):**
1. *Immediately* — Subject: `Hi. We're Rally.` The founding story in 5 sentences (women's sports apparel = sized-down men's clothes with worse jokes; we fixed it). 10% first-order code.
2. *Day 2* — Subject: `The shirt that ends the argument.` One product story (mansplain/beer tee), UGC frames, the "she talks directly to him" line-up.
3. *Day 5* — Subject: `Lucky is a strategy.` The Lucky collection + COMES OFF WHEN WE WIN. + (RUB FOR LUCK). Gift angle footer for male readers: "Buying for her? Skip the roses jersey. Get the joke right."

**Post-purchase (order confirmation +7 days):** review ask + the survey below + UGC prompt ("wear it, tag us, best screenshot each week gets next drop free").

## 4. Post-purchase survey (the acquisition-story KPI)

Three questions, one screen, in the +7day email (and/or Shopify post-purchase page):
1. **Who's this for?** Me / Gift for her / Gift for him / Other → **KPI: % "Me" from female buyers — the number the brand's acquirers will ask for.** Baseline it from day 1.
2. **How'd you find us?** TikTok / IG / friend / search / other → channel attribution for the calendar.
3. **What should we print next?** free text → the bench list feeds itself.

Track monthly: % female self-purchase, repeat rate, UGC volume, list growth. Those four numbers ARE the acquisition story ("we're selling our audience").
